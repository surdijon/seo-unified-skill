## Domain Profile Diagnosis

Before choosing a strategy, diagnose which dimension of authority is weakest:

| Dimension | What it measures | Signs of weakness | Strategy |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|----------|
| **Citation** | Inbound references from quality sources | Few referring domains, low-authority linkers, no editorial links | Guest posts, resource page outreach, broken link building |
| **Identity** | Brand recognition and entity coherence | No branded search, no knowledge panel, inconsistent NAP | PR, Wikipedia/Wikidata, industry directories, podcast appearances |
| **Trust** | Legitimacy and manipulation absence | Spammy profile, no HTTPS, thin content, manual actions | Disavow toxic links first, then earn editorial links from authoritative publishers |
| **Eminence** | Visibility and industry standing | Low organic traffic, no SERP features, no media mentions | Original research, conference speaking, expert commentary |

## Strategies (Ranked by Effectiveness)

### Tier 1: High Authority, Scalable

**Original Research & Data:** Publish original surveys, studies, industry reports. Journalists cite original data naturally. No one else has this data.

**Free Tools & Calculators:** Build genuinely useful tools. People link to tools they use and recommend. Ongoing utility = ongoing links.

**Comprehensive Guides (10x Content):** Create the definitive resource on a topic. Must be genuinely better than everything on page 1. Update regularly.

### Tier 2: Targeted, Relationship-Based

**Guest Posting (Quality):** Write for relevant, authoritative publications. Focus on sites your audience reads. 1 link on a relevant authoritative site > 10 links on generic blogs.

**Resource Page Link Building:** Search `[topic] + "resources"` / `[topic] + "useful links"`. Offer genuinely useful content for inclusion.

**Broken Link Building:** Find broken outbound links on relevant sites. Create content that replaces the dead resource. Contact site: "I noticed a broken link, here's a working alternative."

### Tier 3: Community & PR

**Journalist Query Platforms:** Connectively, Qwoted, Featured. Monitor for relevant source requests. Provide expert quotes. Fast response = critical.

**Community Participation:** Reddit, forums, Slack groups. Share expertise not links. Links come naturally when people discover your content.

**Digital PR:** Newsjack trending topics with data/commentary. Create content tied to seasonal events. Pitch to journalists with data-backed angles.

## Linkable Asset Types (What Earns Links)

| Asset Type | Link Earning Potential | Effort to Create | Best For |
|-----------|----------------------|-----------------|----------|
| Original research/survey | Very high | High (needs data collection) | B2B, SaaS, marketing |
| Free tool/calculator | Very high | High (needs development) | SaaS, finance, health |
| Definitive guide (10x) | High | High (needs comprehensive content) | Any informational niche |
| Infographic/data visualization | Medium-high | Medium (needs design) | Visual niches, B2B |
| Template/checklist (downloadable) | Medium | Low-medium | SaaS, marketing, productivity |
| Expert roundup | Medium | Low-medium | B2B, thought leadership |
| Case study with data | Medium | Medium | B2B, agency, SaaS |
| Interactive tool/quiz | High | High | Consumer niches |
| Industry report (annual) | Very high | High (ongoing) | Any B2B niche |
| Glossary/encyclopedia | Medium | Medium | Educational niches |

**Rule:** If you can't create original assets, focus on broken link building + resource page outreach.

## Internal Linking Best Practices

- Important pages should have 3+ incoming internal links
- Use descriptive, varied anchor text (not all exact-match keywords)
- Hub-and-spoke structure for topic clusters
- No broken internal links
- Every page should be reachable within 3 clicks from homepage
- Check for orphan pages (zero internal links pointing to them)
